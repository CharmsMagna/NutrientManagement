What is Water Saving in Perpetuity?

  • Retrofitting existing properties with water-efficient fixtures
  • Mandating water efficiency in new developments
  • Encouraging behaviour change through education and incentives
  • Ensuring long-term monitoring and reporting on water-saving measures

What is Temporary Water Saving?

Temporary water saving is to alleviate short-term abstraction pressure & wastewater treatment plant upgrades...

  • Water efficiency campaigns targeting high-consumption areas
  • Temporary water restrictions during drought periods
  • Accelerated investment in wastewater treatment plant upgrades
  • Deployment of smart water metering and leakage reduction initiatives

Why is Nutrient Neutrality Different from Water Neutrality?

Aspect Nutrient Neutrality Water Neutrality
Objective Prevent additional nutrient pollution Ensure developments do not increase water demand
Key Drivers Habitats Regulations Assessment, Water Framework Directive Water scarcity, local water availability
Mitigation Measures Wetlands, SuDS, wastewater treatment Water efficiency retrofits, smart meters
Outcome Protects aquatic habitats Reduces abstraction pressure
